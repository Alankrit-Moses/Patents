A trajectory shift pattern is a temporal pattern in which an entity changes its direction, speed, or status after a specific time or event. The entity may shift from slow growth to rapid growth, growth to stagnation, growth to decline, decline to recovery, dominance to slowdown, niche activity to momentum, or one technological pathway to another. The pattern requires an explicit before/after structure or a stated turning point; a simple statement that something is growing, declining, dominant, or important is not sufficient.
