A growth divergence pattern is a relational temporal pattern in which two comparable entities move farther apart over a shared time interval. It captures cases where one country, technology, organization, application area, or other category pulls away from another, or where one grows while the other remains flat or declines. The pattern requires a common measure and time interval for both entities, an explicit or implicit comparison between them, and tabular evidence that the difference between their values is larger at the end of the interval than at the beginning. A simple difference in CAGR, rank, final value, or absolute size is not sufficient unless it corresponds to a widening gap between the two time series.
