A comparative growth pattern is a temporal pattern in which comparable entities or categories have meaningfully different compound annual growth rates over the same period. It identifies faster-growing, slower-growing, stagnant, or declining entities independently of their absolute size. The pattern requires a shared measure and time interval for every entity. Unlike a trajectory shift, it does not require a turning point; unlike a top-k pattern, it compares entities by annualized relative growth rather than their final absolute values.
